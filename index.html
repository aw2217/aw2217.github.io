So uni is over - What now?

Read time: 2 minutes

Haven’t seen some of your uni mates since the dizzy heights of graduation? Job applications only met with ‘we regret to inform you’ and ‘I’m sorry to say’? The history-steeped walls of your childhood bedroom becoming all too familiar?

You’re not the only one.

I think if I had to sum up the past couple of months in a few words, I’d say ‘sub-optimal’ doesn’t quite cut it.

I for one naively thought I’d be a proper adult when I finished university. I thought I’d have my next steps planned out. I thought I’d be eager to move onto the next stage in a seamless transition. I thought everything would be straighforward. Moving back in with my parents with no plans and no job wasn’t quite what I had in mind! It sometimes feels like everyone else has things all figured out and you’re the only one who doesn’t.

But that’s why I’ve decided to create this page.

I want to remind myself and everyone else in the same boat (and there are many thousands of us in that same boat) that everything we’re feeling is completely normal and, more importantly, that it’s ok.

It’s ok to feel frustrated. It’s ok to feel like there’s little direction in your current endeavors. It’s ok if everything all feels a bit too much to manage.

Over the coming months, as so many of us are united in this challenging – but temporary – period of our lives, I’ll be documenting what’s helping me keep pushing on and what we can do to make the most out of this unusal period in our lives.

But for now - I thought I'd end with the words I remind myself of when I'm feeling a bit down - 'Keep plodding on! You're doing better than you think'.

Author: Alex Weaver
